Synopsis

Laplacian eigenmap is an image segmentation algorithm that began to gain traction in recent years. It involves a generalized eigenvalue problem which extracts high-level features from local neighborhood information. Unfortunately, it is computationally costly to compute eigenvalues of a large linear systems. We make use of general-purpose GPUs to accelerate the segmentation process.
